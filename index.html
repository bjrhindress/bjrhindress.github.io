Brian Rhindress

Hi, I'm Brian - a computer engineer, data scientist, and law student driven to develop technology and policy for the public good. I've journeyed through non-profits, government, companies, and grassroots organizing, focusing on issues like public transportation, algorithmic bias, labor, and housing rights. In academia, my interests have spanned the micro and macro, from innovation and entrepreneurship to regional economic development and antitrust law. I love finding innovative ways to address complex challenges facing real people, fueled by listening to a diversity of perspectives, collaborative solutioning, persistence, and introspection.

Current hyper-focus:

Lately, my thoughts have been centered around the dual-edged potential of generative AI, predictive analytics, and the automation of legal tools and workflows. I believe these developments could either close or further expand the justice gap. We should fight to close it.

If you feel we share common interests or could collaborate, please reach out!
